# Story Dossier — *Murder at Magnolia Manor*

## Genre
Cozy mystery

## Setting
**Magnolia Manor** — a restored antebellum bed-and-breakfast in Savannah, Georgia. The manor sits on a quiet square shaded by live oaks, Spanish moss, and azalea hedges. The kitchen is the social hub; the wraparound porch is where secrets slip out over sweet tea.

**Time period:** Present day, early October. The annual Savannah Garden Tour is three days away.

## Protagonist
**Josephine "Josie" Bellefleur**, 38. Former corporate event planner from Atlanta who inherited Magnolia Manor from her great-aunt Eulalie six months ago. Josie is sharp, warm, chronically over-scheduled, and still learning how to run a B&B. She has a habit of stress-baking pecan tarts and talking to her great-aunt's portrait when she's thinking through a problem.

**Motivation:** Prove she can make the manor profitable before the bank review in December. Solving a murder was not on the business plan.

## Supporting Cast

- **Thibault "Tibby" Marchand**, 72 — the manor's longtime groundskeeper and Eulalie's oldest friend. Knows every hidden room and grudge in the neighborhood. Speaks in proverbs. Keeps a flask of bourbon in his gardening vest.
- **Detective Rae Kimura**, 41 — Savannah PD. Methodical, dry-humored, skeptical of amateur sleuths. Respects Josie but will not share case files.
- **Clementine Ashford**, 65 — president of the Savannah Garden Society and the victim's business partner. Imperious, competitive, and very interested in buying Magnolia Manor.
- **Hugo Voss**, 45 — a visiting horticulturist from Charleston. Charming, evasive, has a past connection to the victim that he downplays.
- **Darla Roux**, 28 — Josie's part-time front-desk clerk and aspiring true-crime podcaster. Enthusiastic, occasionally reckless with information.

## Victim
**Percival "Percy" Fontaine**, 68. Semi-retired antiques dealer, Garden Society treasurer, and Clementine's silent partner. Found dead in the manor's greenhouse at 6:14 a.m. by Tibby. Cause of death: poisoning — a toxic compound extracted from oleander blossoms mixed into his nightly chamomile tea.

## Central Mystery
Who poisoned Percy Fontaine, and why, two days before the Garden Tour that would have publicly revealed the Society's financial irregularities?

## Story Arc (6 Chapters)

| Ch | Title | Key Event |
|----|-------|-----------|
| 1 | *A Body Among the Blooms* | Tibby discovers Percy's body. Josie secures the greenhouse. Detective Kimura arrives. Josie overhears Clementine's too-calm phone call. |
| 2 | *Thorns and Tea Leaves* | Josie finds oleander clippings in the potting shed. Darla reveals Percy was about to expose a financial discrepancy. Hugo deflects questions about his past visits. |
| 3 | *Root Rot* | Josie discovers Percy changed his will the previous week. Tibby shares a decades-old grudge between Percy and Eulalie. Clementine offers to buy the manor — aggressively. |
| 4 | *Deadheading* | The Garden Tour proceeds under tension. Josie finds Hugo's real reason for being in Savannah: Percy owed him a significant debt. Darla's podcast episode leaks a detail only the killer would know. |
| 5 | *Frost Warning* | The killer attempts to destroy evidence. Josie pieces together the timeline using the manor's guest register and Tibby's garden journal. Kimura reluctantly accepts Josie's theory. |
| 6 | *Full Bloom* | Confrontation at the Garden Tour gala. The killer is revealed. Josie saves the manor's reputation — and its future. |

## Themes
- Inheritance (property, secrets, obligations)
- Community vs. self-interest
- The cost of keeping up appearances

## Tone and Voice
Warm but suspenseful. First-person narration with Josie's wry internal commentary. Sensory details (food, flowers, weather) ground every scene. Humor comes from character, never from plot. Death is treated seriously even when the setting is cozy.

## Chapter 1 — Specific Beats
1. **Opening image:** Josie is up before dawn, prepping breakfast. The kitchen smells like cinnamon and coffee. She's rehearsing her Garden Tour welcome speech to the cat.
2. **Inciting incident:** Tibby bursts through the back door — "Miss Josie, you better come to the greenhouse."
3. **Discovery:** Percy is slumped in the wicker chair beside the potting bench. A teacup is overturned on the flagstones. Oleander petals are scattered around him.
4. **Reaction:** Josie calls 911, secures the scene, keeps guests away from the greenhouse wing.
5. **Detective arrives:** Kimura takes over. She's efficient, polite, and clearly uninterested in Josie's theories.
6. **First clue:** While Kimura interviews Tibby, Josie overhears Clementine on the porch phone: "Well, that simplifies things considerably."
7. **Chapter close:** Josie stares at the greenhouse from the kitchen window, pecan tart dough going cold in her hands, thinking: *Great-Aunt Eulalie, what did you leave me?*
